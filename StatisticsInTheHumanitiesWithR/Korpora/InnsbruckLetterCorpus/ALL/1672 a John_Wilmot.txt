Madam I am extreamly troubled for the sickness of yr son as well in consideration of the affliction it gives you as the dearness I have for him myself you have I heare done mee the favour to expect me long in the Country where I intended to have bin long agoe but Court affaires are more hardly sollicited now then ever and having follow'd them till I had spent all my owne money & y rs too I was forc't to stay somthing longer here till I had contriv'd a supply w ch being now dispatch'd I have nothing to hinder mee from what I heartily desire w ch is to waite on yr La sp at Adderbury I am yr humble servant Rochester For my Wife
