Syr I most hertely commend me vn to you and where as I haue byn somewhat sloo in wrytyng syne I arryuyd hyther to our cuntrey (where as I bere the ayre bettur then I dyd wyth you in Italy) I wyl now my slaknes therin by the lenghthe of thys in some parte recom pense the wych I trvst schal no thyng offend you but bryng to you grete plesure and comfort Syr as you know syth our fyrst acquynt ance and famylyaryte many letturys ther hath byn at sundry tymys betwyx vs wryten and much commvnycacyon ther hath byn also con cernyng the Instytution of our lyfys wyth such fydelyte mynystryd apon both partys as was conuenyent to the syncerenes of our hartys and myndys but yf euer any of thes you haue jugyd to be worthy of remembrance or yet dow styke in your memory and mynd I besech you let thes few wordys wych I now to you wryte be put in the nombur of them wyth gud aduertysement and consyderacyon of the same for they concerne the hole ordur of your lyfe here aftur to be lade in thys our cuntrey among your natural louerys and frendys Syr I was but Late by the synguler gentylnes of Maystur Secretary wos gudnes bothe toward me and also to you ys so sonke in to my brest that duryng my lyfe I schal euer reken my selfe next to our prynce aboue al other most faythfully to hym bovnden for in the court to the kyngys seruyce and by hys most louyng commendacyonys so gracyovsly of hys hyghnes acceptyd and admyttyd that schortly aftur hyt plesyd the same to cal me to hys presence and ther of you of your studys and of your sentence and opynyon in hys gracys most weyghty causys here late defynyd most louyngly many thyngys to demande to the wych I made such answere as euer I haue jugyd conuenyent to be made before the maiesty of a prynce that ys such thyngys as I knowe manyfest and true playnly to affyrme and such wherof I stond in dowte by coniecture only to reherce and so your mynd hart and desyre to dow hys grace true and faythful seruyce wych I know no other wyse then I know myn owne I boldly dyd affyrme but as touchyng your opynyon in hys gracys late defynyd causys one of the matrymony the other concernyng the authoryte of the pope for as much as you euer haue vsyd thys prudent sylence neuer to dysclose your sentence and mynd but in tyme and place I coude not of your opynyon any thyng therin playnly affyrme but yet thys to hys hyghnes I sayd and suerly I thought that as fer as your lernyng and jugement wych I estymyd by tyme and dylygent study somewhat was alteryd and incresyd also touchyng the dycernyng betwyx goddys law and mannys wold streche and extend al your powar and al such knolege and lernyng as by the gudnes of god and hys gracys lyberalyte you had obtaynyd and got to the mayntenyng of such thyngys as hys gracys wysedome by court of parlyament therin had decred you wold gladly confer to the honowre of hys hyghnes and welth of hys reame thys much I sayd thys fer I went but hys grace not satysfyd therwyth desyryng to haue your sentence therin playnly declaryd commandyd me thys now to wryte to you that hys plesure was that you schold lyke a lernyd man al assertyon by any cause rysyng set asyde in thos ij causys pondur and wey the nature of the thyngys as they be in them selfe and puttyng a parte al sucessys and dangerovse effectys wych of them may insue leuying al such thyngys to hys gracys wysedome and hys pollycy declare your sentence truly and playn wythout coloure or cloke of dyssymulatyon (wych hys grace most pryncely abhorryth) not wyllyng you of thes thyngys to make any grete volume or boke but breuely to geddur the most effectual resonys wych in your stomake be of most weyght and them to set forth aftur your playn fascyon and maner of wrytyng thys was hys gracys plesure and commandement that I schold to you wryte wych I haue as nere as my memory wold serue me therin truly and faythfylly now to you exercysyd now syr considur and prudently wey how pryncely a request thys ys of oure prynce and then I am sure you wyl imploy your selfe wyth al dylygence and study to satisfye hys nobul desyre to the wych also mastur Secretary (whose most louyng gudnes toward you gyuyth place to no man) mostly gentyly doth exhort you wyllyng you also in any case what so euer your sentence in thes causys schal be to vse your wont and custumyd playnes wyth prudent symplycyte and me apon hys behalfe thys to certyfye you that in case be your lernyng and jugement in thes materys of weyght wold strech and extend to the satysfying of the kyngys desyre and mynd that then your retorne hither to your cvntrey schold be gretely to the kyngys plesure to your owne comfort and much profyt to the rest of your frendys ye and yet ferther yf so be that your knolege and lernyng wold not serue you to thys purpos and request of the kyng yet notwythstondyng wold he aduyse you of a tendur and louyng mynd to prepare yourselfe at your conuenyent leser toward your cuntrey dowtyng no thyng but that the kyngys hyghnes in other hys causys and hys affayrys schal vse your seruyce and most louyng and seruysabul mynd for sory he ys that ther among stravngerys wythout profyt to your cuntrey your vertues schold be so utturly drownyd and lyke as in a dreme vanysch away wherby syr you may playnly perceyue the gentilnes of hys stomake and synguler gudnes to al men of honesty wych to you almost vnacquaynted and of smal famylyaryte beryth suche mynd mouyd only by the opynyon of vertue wych to hym fame hath reportyd in so much that thys he wyllyd me now by my letturys of hys gudnes and beneuolent mynd so to assure you that in al such thyngys as myght touche your preferment to my lady your mother and my lord your brother whome nature so straytly byndyth only he wold gyue place wherin he schowyth so gentyl a stomake that I dare thys boldly now say that yf euer hereaftur hyt schalbe your chaunce presently here of thys mynd in hym experyence to take you schal as I dow for hys vertues and not only for hys authoryte haue hym in stabyl and reuerent loue such ys hys wysedome and in materys of state hys hygh pollycy and thvs now you haue hard the most prudent aduyse and synguler beneuolence of mastur Secretary to the wych I dowte not but that wyth grete gladnes you wyl apply yourselfe wyllyng therby to satysfye our pryncys plesure and desyre And now syr for by cause syth our last departure out of our cuntrey lytlyl commvnycacyon concern yng thes materys hathe byn betwyx vs had I wyl now adioyne thes few wordys vn to you  Pondur you wel thys leuytycal law and how hyt ys rotyd in the law of nature and how by general conseyl hyt hath byn many tymys delcaryd and authorysyd therby and forther how apon the other syde the sklendurnes of thys long vsurpyd and abvsyd authoryte of the pope wych by pacyence of pryncys simplycyte of the pepul and ambycyovse auaryce of hys predecessorys in processe of tyme by lytyl and lytyl ys growen to thys intollerabul iniquyte and then I thynk that you schal see in thes cavsys the jugyd truth and playn equyte But al thys I leue to your owne consyderacyon and jugement praying to hym of whome to al men cumyth al lyght that by hys lyght and grace you may see the truth and so then to set hyt forth that hyt may be comfort to our prynce plesure to your selfe and to al other here your louarys and frendys And thys now syr I wyl make an end fynychyng my letturys wyth comfortabul newys that al such rumor and fame wych by men of corrupt jugement not hauyng dyscretyon to juge and dyscerne betwyx veray and true relygyon and lyght and false superstycyon syth was in Italy you dyuulgyd ys vtterly false and ful of vanyte For of this dowte you nothyng that albehyt apon many resonabul and ivste causys our most nobul prynce hathe wyth drawen hymselfe from the popys authoryte yet from the certayn and sure groundys of scrypture hys grace in no poynt ys slyde no nor yet from the lawys nor ceremonys of the church the wych yet stond in ful strenghth and authoryte and so they schal boldly I dare affyrme vntyl such tyme that to hys hyghnes and to hys most wyse conseyl hyt schal appere expedyent them to abrogate and other to substytute by commyn assent more agreabul to thys tyme and to the nature of our men and also to our hole cuntry more conuenyent here ys no thyng downe wythout due ordur and resonabul mean here ys touchyng relygyon nothyng almost alteryd at al but that wych was of al other most necessary wych ys as I trust and schalbe a veray ground and a foundatyon to cyuyle ordur and a true and ryght pollycy thys ys the state here and of thys one thyng I dow you assure yf I had found truth in dede thes thyngys wych by mysreport ther wyth you were commynly sayd as that our prynce schold be slypt also from the groundys of scrypture from the honowre of the sacramentys and from al the commyn Lawys and holsome ceremonys of the church wythout ordur I wold neuer haue byn so wythout sense or stomake of an honest man as at thys tyme to haue sought to entur to hys seruyce for the desyre wych I haue long nuryschyd in my brest to serue thys our mastur and prynce ys in thys stabyl and I trust euer schalbe in hys seruyce to serue god and my cuntrey to the wych purpos the rest of my lyfe I wyl now dedycate to hys grace and wyth such hart and mynd serue hym wythal as ys conuenyent to a true faythful and chrystyan subiect toward hys most nobul and catholyke prynce thys ys my mynd and I am sure the same ys yourys the wych I trvst in factys you schal haue place schortly to declare and thys I commyt you to god At London the xv of February By yourys assuryd Thomas Starkey Endorsed Thomas Starkey to his frend in Italy wishing him to geve his opinyone to the kinges grace touching his oppiniyone for the Altering of Relygeon and the Abolishing
